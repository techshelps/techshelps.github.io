Number of Uplink EGPRS TBF Abnormal Releases due to channel preempt

Measurement Counter

A9217: UP_EGPRS_TBF_ABNORM_REL_CHAN_PREEMPT

Description

This measurement provides the number of uplink EGPRS TBF abnormal releases due to channel preemption in a granularity period. A high measurement value is yielded because congestion occurs on the Abis interface or the dynamic PDCHs in use are occupied by the CS services. Refer to the related information such as device alarms to locate the cause.

Unit

Integer number or integer.

Measurement Point

If a PDCH is occupied by the MS for CS services with a higher priority, or congestion occurs on the Abis interface, then the BSC releases the PDCH irrespective of the MS status. The uplink EGPRS TBF that uses the PDCH as the control channel is also released.

When the MS is in transmission state and the uplink EGPRS TBF exists, the BSC releases the uplink EGPRS TBF that uses the channel as the control channel if the channel assigned to the MS is preempted and thus no channel is available for the uplink EGPRS TBF.

Figure 1 shows the procedure of uplink EGPRS TBF abnormal release due to channel preemption. Each time the channel is preempted and thus the BSC abnormally releases the uplink EGPRS TBF that uses the channel as the control channel (see measurement point A), the counter is incremented by one.
