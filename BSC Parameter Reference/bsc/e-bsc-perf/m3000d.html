Call Drops due to ERR IND Received on SDCCH in Stable State

Measurement Counter

M3000D:CELL_CALL_DROPS_ERR_IND_ORIGIN_CALL_SD

M3000E:CELL_CALL_DROPS_ERR_IND_PAGING_RES_SD

M3000F: ELL_CALL_DROPS_ERR_IND_RE_ESTABLISH_SD

M3000G:CELL_CALL_DROPS_ERR_IND_EMERGENCY_SD

M3000H:CELL_CALL_DROPS_ERR_IND_NO_CALL_SD

Description

After the MS seizes a channel, the BTS sends the BSC an ERR IND message, indicating that call drops occur on the Um interface because of the abnormal connection at the radio link layer. This measurement provides the number of call drops on the SDCCH on the basis of the call type (MOC, MTC, Call Re-establishment, Emergency Call, or Non-Call Part).

Unit

Integer number or integer.

Measurement Point

When an SDCCH is seized during a call, the specific counter is incremented by one if the BSC receives an ERR IND message.

The counter is measured at A1, as shown in Figure 1, Figure 2, Figure 3, Figure 4, and Figure 5.
