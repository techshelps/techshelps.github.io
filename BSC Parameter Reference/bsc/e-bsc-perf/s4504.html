Number of the Measurements of the FR VQI in the Customized MR

Measurement Counter

S4504: TRX_EXT_FR_SHORT_VQI_NUM

Description

When decoding the customized extended MR or pre-processed customized extended MR from the BTS, the BSC checks whether the VQI cell is contained in the MS. If the VQI cell is contained, and the MR is from the TCHF, then this counter is incremented by one.

VQI is the MOS score of the voice quality.

When the BTS reports the VQI value, the VQI value is sent to the BSC after being multiplied by 50 and converted into an integer because it is a floating point value. After the BSC receives the VQI value, the VQI value ranges from 0 to 5.1 after being divided by 50. The measurement is performed with VQI 0.5 as a level. The number of VQI 0-0.5 level measurement reports indicates the number of measurement reports with the voice quality smaller than 0.5. Other levels are measured in the same way.

VQI establishes the mapping between radio performance and voice quality and uses the MOS analysis method to provide the rankings of voice quality. MOS is used to assess the quality of the middle- and low-rate voice coding. The MOS value ranges from 1 to 5.
  • 5: Excellent
  • 4: Good
  • 3: Fair
  • 2: Poor
  • 1: Unsatisfactory

Based on the MOS analysis method, the voice quality is further classified into 11 levels for collecting traffic statistics, as listed in table below:

Unit

Integer number or integer.

Measurement Point

After receiving a customized extended MR or pre-processed customized extended MR from the BTS over the TCHH, the BSC decodes the MR. This counter is measured when the VQI cell is contained in the MS.

Formula

This is an original counter without involving any formula.
