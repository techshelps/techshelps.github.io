Call Drops due to Other Causes Received on SDCCH in Stable State

Measurement Counter

M3003A:CELL_CALL_DROPS_OTHER_CAUSE_ORIGIN_CALL_SD

M3003B:CELL_CALL_DROPS_OTHER_CAUSE_PAGING_RES_SD

M3003C:CELL_CALL_DROPS_OTHER_CAUSE_RE_ESTABLISH_SD

M3003D:CELL_CALL_DROPS_OTHER_CAUSE_EMERGENCY_SD

M3003E:CELL_CALL_DROPS_OTHER_CAUSE_NO_CALL_SD

Description

After the MS seizes a channel, if a call is released because of Abis Terrestrial Link Failure, No MRs from MS for a Long Time, Equipment Failure, or Forced Handover Failure, this measurement provides the number of call drops on the SDCCH on the basis of the call type (MOC, MTC, Call Re-establishment, Emergency Call, or Non-Call).

Unit

Integer number or integer.

Measurement Point

The counter of a certain call type is incremented by one in the following situations:

1. The BSC does not receive a measurement report within a certain period (by default, five minutes), and then the BSC sends a CLEAR COMMAND message to the MSC to release the call.

2. The BSC detects a broken RSL and releases all the calls on the RSL.

3. A call drop occurs because of any of the following: TRX mutual aid; dynamic modification of cell attributes, TRX frequencies, or TRX FH data; dynamic deletion of cells or TRXs; blocking of cells, TRXs, or channels.

4. The timer set to wait for the outgoing BSC handover command expires, or the clear request message is received from the MSC before the timer expires.

5. A call drop occurs because of call assignment failure during the handover.

6. The MSC cannot send a short message because of the restriction on sending downlink short messages in data configuration or because of flow control, and then the call is released.

7. A call drop occurs because of software error.

8. A call drop occurs during the emergency handover due to PDCH assignment, or ‚Äèthe handover due to TRX mutual aid, preemption, or blocking of cells, TRXs, and channels.
