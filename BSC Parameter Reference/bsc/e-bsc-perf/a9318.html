Number of Downlink EGPRS intermit transfers

Measurement Counter

A9318: DOWN_EGPRS_TRANS_INTERRUPT_TIMES

Description

This measurement provides the number of intermitted downlink EGPRS transfers in a cell in a granularity period. A high measurement value is yielded because the downlink PDU buffer is emptied upon abnormal downlink EGPRS TBF releases due to SUSPEND, FLUSH, or failed downlink re-establishments due to other causes.

Unit

Integer number or integer.

Measurement Point

The downlink EGPRS transfer may be intermitted in the following situations:

  • The downlink PDU buffer is emptied upon an abnormal downlink EGPRS TBF release due to SUSPEND.

    When a class B MS initiates a CS service request, the BSC stops all the PS services of the MS irrespective of the MS status. When the MS is in transmission state and the downlink EGPRS TBF exists, the MS initiates the suspending procedure by sending the SUSPEND message and disrupts PS services. Accordingly, the BSC releases the downlink EGPRS TBF. If the suspending lasts a long time, the PDU buffer is emptied. Figure 1 shows the procedure for intermitted downlink EGPRS TBF transfer when the MS sends a SUSPEND message to the BSC. When the BSC receives the SUSPEND message, and the suspending lasts a long time (see measurement point A), the counter is incremented by one.

    Figure 1 Intermitted downlink EGRPS transfer due to SUSPEND

  • The downlink PDU buffer is emptied upon an abnormal downlink EGPRS TBF release due to FLUSH.

    When the MS reselects a new cell, the SGSN initiates the cell updating procedure and sends a FLUSH LL PDU to the BSC. Accordingly, the BSC terminates all the PS services of the MS in the original cell irrespective of the MS status. When the MS is in transmission state and the downlink EGPRS TBF exists, the BSC releases the downlink EGPRS TBF and deletes the downlink PDU buffers which are soon expired when receiving the FLUSH LL PDU from the SGSN. Figure 2 shows the procedure of intermitted downlink EGPRS TBF transfer when the BSC receives the FLUSH LL PDU. When the BSC receives the FLUSH LL PDU message (see measurement point A), the counter is incremented by one.

    Figure 2 Intermitted downlink GRPS transfer due to FLUSH

  • The downlink PDU buffer is emptied upon an abnormal downlink EGPRS TBF release due to other causes.

    The downlink PDU buffer is emptied when the re-establishment of downlink EGPRS TBF fails after it is released abnormally due to other causes (such as N3105 overflow, no channel, preempted channel). Figure 3 shows the procedure of intermitted downlink EGPRS TBF transfer due to other causes. When the downlink EGPRS TBF is released (see measurement point A), the counter is incremented by one.

    Figure 3 Intermitted downlink EGPRS transfer due to other causes

Formula

This is an original counter without involving any formula.
