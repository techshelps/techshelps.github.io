Maintaining the BSC

This describes how to maintain the hardware of the BSC6000 on the LMT.

  • Concepts Related to BSC Maintenance
    This describes the basic concepts related to BSC maintenance.
  • Maintaining the BSC Devices
    This describes how to query the status of the devices, how to reset the devices, and how to perform switchover.
  • Maintaining the BSC Interfaces
    This describes how to maintain the BSC interfaces, namely, the Ater, A, Abis, Pb, and Gb interfaces.
  • Maintaining Transmission and Signaling
    This describes how to maintain the signaling links and the ports of interface boards.
  • Maintaining BSC User Resources
    This describes how to maintain the BSC user resources. The user resources consist of port timeslot, interface circuit, TC resources, call resources, DSP speech channel, external speech channel, and internal speech channel.
  • Managing IP Addresses
    This describes how to manage the IP addresses of the devices, IP addresses of the physical ports, IP address routing information, IP addresses of the Ethernet ports, and peer-end IP addresses.
  • Managing the BSC Time
    Managing the BSC time involves managing the time zone, daylight saving time, and BSC system time.
  • Managing the Inventory Information of the BSC
    Inventory management refers to the management based on the equipment archive information. Through inventory management, you can know the running equipment and inventory equipment. You can perform inventory management on either the Local Maintenance Terminal or the M2000.
