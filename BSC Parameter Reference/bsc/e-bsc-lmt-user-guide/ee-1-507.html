Log Type

This describes different types of logs recorded during the normal operation of the BSC.

The BSC logs are classified into the following types:

  • Operation log

    Operation logs record the information about maintenance and configuration operations.

  • Running log

    Running logs record the information about system running status.

  • Debugging log

    Debugging logs record the information about fault location in the BSC.

  • Lastword log

    Lastword logs record the major information, such as timer data, before errors occur in the BSC system. These logs help to locate faults such as abnormal restart.

    The main function of lastword logs is to record and sample the information about board reset. The lastword logs are main resources for system testability and fault location. The lastword logs can provide detailed information for locating faults such as board collapse, and thus improves the efficiency of problem solving.

  • CS CHR log

    The CS CHR logs record the information about CS calls and dot trace to help quickly locate problems.

  • BTS log

    BTS logs record the problems that occur when the BTS is running and the debugging information.

  • Frequency scan log

    Frequency scan logs record the information about the frequency scan of cells. Frequency scan is used for both the BSC maintenance and the network planning.

  • TRX power log

    The TRX power logs record the history TRX power. The logs help to query the TRX power.

  • Equipment room temperature log

    The equipment room temperature logs record the temperature information in the equipment room.
