Examples of Timeslot Assignment in N:1 Multiplexing Mode

This provides the examples of E1 timeslot assignment in 1:1, 2:1, 3:1, and 4:1 multiplexing modes.

NOTE:

The timeslot assignment in each multiplexing mode is based on the following conditions:

  • The speech rate is 16 kbit/s. Four channels of speech occupy one 64 kbit/s timeslot on the Abis interface.
  • For the first TRX, channel 0 (T00C0) is the BCCH and channel 1 (T00C1) is the SDCCH.
  • If the multiplexing mode of a BTS that supports the Flex Abis is 5:1 or 6:1, the BTS must be configured with one ESL.
  • If the multiplexing mode of a BTS that supports the Flex Abis is 5:1, one OML, one ESL, and two RSLs are multiplexed onto one 64 kbit/s timeslot; if the multiplexing mode of a BTS that supports the Flex Abis is 6:1, one OML, one ESL, and two RSLs are multiplexed onto one 64 kbit/s timeslot. The difference between multiplexing modes 5:1, 6:1, and 4:1 lies in the multiplexing of RSLs. The BCCH and SDCCH use RSLs, that is, the timeslots occupied by T00C0 and T00C1 are included in the timeslots occupied by RSLs. Therefore, the 5:1 and 6:1 multiplexing modes are not listed in the following tables.
