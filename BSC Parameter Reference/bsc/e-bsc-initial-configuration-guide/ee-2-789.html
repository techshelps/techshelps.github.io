Configuring the BSC Devices (BM/TC Separated)

This describes how to configure the BSC devices on the LMT when the BM and TC are configured in different subracks.

  1. Adding a GBSR Cabinet
    This describes how to add a GBSR cabinet and configure its attributes through the LMT. When the BM and TC are configured in different subracks, the GBSR cabinet can be configured on the BSC side or on the MSC side. When the GBSR cabinet is configured on the MSC side, the cabinet houses only the GTCS.
  2. Adding a BSC Subrack
    The GMPS is configured by default. You need to configure the GEPS and the GTCS on the LMT.
  3. Configuring a Power Distribution Box
    This describes how to add a power distribution box and configure its attributes through the LMT.
  4. Configuring the BSC Boards
    This describes how to add BSC boards or configure the attributes of the BSC boards. When the BM and TC are configured in different subracks, the GDPUC/GDPUX, GEIUA/GOIUA, GEIUT/GOIUT, GEIUB/GOIUB, GEHUB, GFGUB/GOGUB, GXPUM, GXPUT, GGCU, GTNU, and GSCU must be configured.
  5. Configuring the BSC EAC
    This describes how to configure the EAC for the BSC cabinet. The EAC gathers Boolean values, analog values, and alarm threshold information and reports them to the LMT.
  6. Configuring the Subrack Communication Link
    This describes how to configure the communication links between the GMPS and the GEPS, between GEPSs, or between GTCSs. The links are responsible for the TDM switching between subracks.
