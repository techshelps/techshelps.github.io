Cooperate with Administrators

[This is preliminary documentation and subject to change.]

As more and more software is used in large networked enterprise settings, it is increasingly important to make your application easy to manage. These guidelines relate to making your application manageable:

  • Enable policy settings to uninstall and unadvertise your application. (Required)
  • Provide an .ADM system policy file for your application. (Required)
  • Provide for disabling Run and Find dialogs. (Required)
  • Support NoViewContextMenu in shell extensions. (Required)
  • Call ShellExecute instead of CreateProcess. (Required)
  • Support roaming users. (Best Practice)
  • Do not collect single-user data during installation. (Best Practice)
  • Support administrative additions during network install. (Recommended)
  • Generate a disk image for multiple installs during network installation. (Recommended)
  • Install shared files in shared locations during network installation. (Recommended)
  • Support silent installs and push strategies during network installation. (Recommended)
  • Do not require a local footprint during network install. (Recommended)
  • Provide a diagnostic logging option. (Recommended)
  • Do not assume write access to a user's machine. (Recommended)
  • Support dynamic policy changes by handling WM_SETTINGCHANGE messages. (Recommended)
  • Expose private policy keys where appropriate. (Best Practice)
