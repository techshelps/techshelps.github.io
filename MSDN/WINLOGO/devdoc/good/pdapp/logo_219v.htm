Separate Add-On Products

[This is preliminary documentation and subject to change.]

Add-on products are accessory products, such as wizards, templates, and macros, that are not executable files.

A separate add-on product is packaged and marketed separately from the product it supports. If a wizard, template, or macro is always packaged only with the 32-bit product it supports, it is considered part of that product for purposes of the Designed for Microsoft® Windows® Logo testing.

Special Requirement for Add-On Products

  • Host an add-on product on 32-bit products that are logo-compliant. (Required)

General Requirements

An add-on product, by definition, does not have to provide an executable file (.exe). With that exception, add-on products must follow all Designed for Microsoft® Windows® Logo requirements that apply to any functionality they provide. For example:

  • Any executable code included in add-on products must be 32-bit code.
  • Add-on products must provide automated installers and uninstallers that follow user-interface guidelines.
  • Add-on products that use native data file types must register them and their icons.
  • Add-on products that use the mouse or keyboard, or that draw on the screen must support the Accessibility requirements.
