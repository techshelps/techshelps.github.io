The Shape of Platforms to Come

Developer Network trims number of CDs in U.S., Canada

John Rae-Grant

July 13, 1995

Here's how rumors start: First, we at the Developer Network decide "Hey, let's ask subscribers what they think about the Developer Network."

Then we have to decide what to ask and how to ask it. (Don't laugh, this isn't as easy as it sounds.) Once we've made that hurdle, we prepare to solicit your feedback. We hold focus groups. We send out surveys. We knock on doors. We keep our eyes open for two-yolk eggs and strangely shaped clouds.

Then, like wildfire, the whispers—soon to transmogrify into a thunderous roar—start. "Pssst, the Developer Network is going to be, gulp, changing."

Uncertainty and suspicion ensue. I guess that's natural. After all, change can be, well, unsettling. The current situation may have its little problems and hiccups, but at least you kind of know what to expect. We here at the Developer Network are in the same boat when it comes to change. We know that while our current offerings don't satisfy all your needs and sometimes engender complaints, they do work for a large number of subscribers.

But we are also faced with the reality that we have to change. How else can we keep you up-to-date? It was a huge change when we made the Development Platform available through our Level 2 membership. It was a major step when we started publishing Developer Network News and technical information on the World Wide Web (and now The Microsoft Network). It was change when we started dedicating more attention to Visual Basic and OLE.

Does that mean everybody is happy with our changes? Probably not. Does that mean we don't make mistakes? Probably not. Does that mean we should stop trying to improve our program? Definitely not. If we didn't change, we'd still be selling copies of Microsoft QuickBasic and Typing Tutor door-to-door.

Still, you have a perfect right to be thinking, "Now they're gonna remove everything I care about from the Development Platform and leave only that stupid checkbook sample! Then they're gonna make me pay through the nose each week for each individual bit."

Well, I'm here to tell you, "Not so." (Oh, I forgot to introduce myself. I'm John Rae-Grant. How are ya?)

We are going to tune Level 2. The reason is pretty simple. It's TOO BIG! Many of you told us not only do you not want many of the platform contents, but also that you think we are holding back on good stuff because it's TOO BIG!

One Level 2 member thoughtfully shared his pain with us in this way: "I'm just not getting the use I thought I would out of the Finnish version of Windows NT 3.5 ever since that CAD/CAM numerical process control project for Santa's Workshop fell through."

Our Development Platform is in danger of gravitational collapse due to sheer volume. So, we plan to remove some less popular stuff from CDs that Level 2 members in the United States and Canada automatically receive each quarter. (In other countries, local subsidiaries will decide whether and how to implement any changes.)

By separating out the international versions of the operating systems (12 CDs) and the device driver kits (3 CDs), we can trim the number of Platform CDs from the current 19 to about 4.

This change won't be fully implemented until the October release because we couldn't get our act together to save all these discs in July. You'll notice, however, that the July Platform contains three separate packs:

• U.S. pack—U.S. versions of Windows and Windows NT Workstation operating systems (retail and debug, when available), resource kits, software development kits (SDKs), and the Setup program.

• International pack—International versions of Windows and Windows NT Workstation operating systems, SDKs, and SDK extensions.

• DDK pack—U.S. and international versions of device driver kits (DDKs) and DDK extensions.

Does this mean you can no longer get the Finnish version of Windows NT 3.5 if you want? No. We are making everything we removed available through optional add-on programs. This means you'll have to let us know if you want to keep receiving anything that we've removed from the basic Level 2 package. Within a month or so after you receive the July release, we'll be sending out a card explaining how to "qualify" yourself for the International and/or DDK pack.

As the new "muckamuck" around here, my goal is a program as responsive and successful as possible, so please keep that feedback coming. We really are listening.

John "Johnny the Fish" Rae-Grant is the new business unit manager of the Microsoft Developer Network.
