CompuServe: A Gateway to Internet Resources

July 13, 1995

The Internet provides a wealth of technical resources and information for developers. There is a wide variety of ways to delve into this gold mine of e-mail lists and news groups. One of the most comprehensive is through CompuServe NetLauncher, which offers full access to the Internet using any browser that supports point-to-point protocol (PPP) and to the World Wide Web through SPRY Mosaic.

CompuServe members can download the NetLauncher software free (GO INTERNET). To use NetLauncher, you need to run WinCIM version 1.3 or later, or CompuServe Navigator for Windows. If you are not currently a CompuServe member and would like to explore the Internet through CompuServe, call (800) 524-3388 from within the U.S. or (614) 529-1340 from outside the U.S., and ask for Operator 230.

CompuServe's US$9.95 monthly basic service fee now includes three hours of Internet access per month. An optional "Internet Services Club" offers 20 hours a month for US$24.95. Detailed information on pricing can be found on CompuServe at GO INTERNET.

Usenet news groups

You may want to check out the many developer-related Usenet (Net News) news groups, which are peer-to-peer discussion forums centered on particular areas of interest. In addition, Usenet provides FAQs—lists of frequently asked questions and answers, maintained by third parties. The Microsoft Windows Programmer FAQ contains developer information, and the Microsoft Windows FAQ contains user information. These FAQs are available in either ASCII text or Windows Help file formats.
