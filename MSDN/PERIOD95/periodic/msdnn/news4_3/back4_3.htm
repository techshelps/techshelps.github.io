Backtalk: A Continuing Dialog on the Development Library and Platform

Melinda Hohlbein

May 9, 1995

Miss me? I'm not out there yakking with you all online right now because I had a baby. And you know, a total stranger had the nerve to ask me if it hurt. Yeah, it hurt. Go pass a kidney stone while undergoing abdominal surgery—without anesthesia—and having your fingernails pulled out with pliers and then we'll talk. Of course, the baby is kind of cute.

But back to business.

An individual—with clearly too much time on his hands—wrote to us recently and recommended that I put more "personality" in my writing. Note he didn't mention my personality, just more personality. Really? Okay, but I couldn't find a place to put unmentionable phrases in any of these sections, so my shameless display of character will have to wait until the next issue.

Speaking of character, we had some lively and wholly unmedicated ones write in these past few months demanding that we address their concerns. Read on to find out the sordid details. I know you're breathless.

Browser: The next generation

The demands are already rolling in for the next generation of the browser software employed by the Development Library CD-ROM. We're planning to release it sometime later this year. (Is that vague enough?)

I asked Bill Shelby—browser team developer extraordinaire—to give us some hints about what we can expect in the next version of the browser. Here's what he said: "We came up with a fix that allows you to search and find anything by just thinking at the Find dialog box, and we tripled the capacity of the disc, but we accidentally formatted the drive with some (not backed up) source code when we really needed more disk space for something else."

You see what I have to work with. Thanks to all of you who have sent in suggestions about the Library—keep 'em coming, please.

Get FAQ

Abel Watchford of Cranston, Rhode Island, writes: "You must get asked this all the time, but what..."

Abe's question isn't the issue here, but he raised a great point. Why don't we have a central location for answers to common questions (technical and nontechnical) about the Developer Network? Abe, Abe, buddy, it exists. The Developer Network frequently asked questions (FAQ) help and text files are ready and waiting for you to download from the following locations:

• ftp microsoft.com: /developr/msdn/faq

• CompuServe: GO MSDN, Library 1

• Here on the Internet OffRamp, under Developer Information: http://www.microsoft.com/pages/developer/msdn/
offramp/msdninfo/msdnfaq.htm

• The Microsoft Network: open Categories/Computers & Software/Software/Microsoft/Microsoft Developer Network/Library Sampler or Platform Overview

Plug me into Windows 95

Eric Baird, of a town that shall remain nameless, espoused to us the virtues of a "one-stop notice board for official Windows 95 status updates."

Can you say WinNews? Sure, I knew you could. Here are your options:

• E-mail: to subscribe to WinNews, send mail to enews@microsoft.nwnet.com with the words SUBSCRIBE WINNEWS as the sole text of your message

• WinNews Servers: on the Internet, use ftp or the World Wide Web: ftp://ftp.microsoft.com/PerOpSys/Win_News, http://www.microsoft.com

• The Microsoft Network: open Categories/Computers & Software/Software/Microsoft/Windows 95/WinNews

• CompuServe: GO WINNEWS

• Prodigy: JUMP WINNEWS

• America Online: keyword WINNEWS

• GEnie: download files from the WinNews area under the Windows RTC
