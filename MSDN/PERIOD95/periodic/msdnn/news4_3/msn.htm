Target: The Microsoft Network

Developer Network does double duty online

Handan Selamoglu

May 9, 1995

It was a perfect match. The first time we saw The Microsoft Network (MSN), the new online service included with Windows 95, we knew it offered an excellent opportunity to expand and complement our existing CD-ROM and printed publications from the Developer Network.

MSN is sleek. It is elegant. It is nimble. One click from the Windows 95 shell gives you immediate access to a whole set of services—chat areas, bulletin boards, worldwide e-mail exchange, and, most importantly, a rich online-publishing environment—the perfect place, we thought, to communicate with developers and to publish up-to-date technical information. The Developer Network thus became one of the first content providers on MSN—the proud owner of the new Developer Network forum.

To access our new area on MSN, click the Microsoft Developer Network icon located in Categories/Computers & Software/Software/Microsoft. Double-click the icons in the window to open the titles and services listed.

Building on past online experience

As most of you know, this is not our first foray into the online arena. In addition to our dedicated CompuServe forum (GO MSDN) and share on the Microsoft ftp server (ftp://ftp.microsoft.com/developr/msdn), we recently launched the Developer Network OffRamp on the World Wide Web (http://www.microsoft.com, and click on Developer Network OffRamp). Now the Developer Network forum on MSN gives us the opportunity to expand our online presence and to deliver information and news to a larger number of developers.

Over the last few months, we've started working with other groups at Microsoft to populate the Developer Network forum with useful information and services for our members and for other developers. We've defined seven major sections, which provide the information listed below. (These sections are currently in the prototype stage and contain only a few articles.)

News. An online version of Microsoft Developer Network News. However, this section provides much more than a sampling of news, features, and columns from the printed version of the newspaper. Posting on MSN allows us to bypass the space and scheduling restrictions of print-based media, and gives us the opportunity to post updates, news, and event notifications on a weekly, daily, even hourly basis.

Products. Data sheets, specifications, demos, and related information on Microsoft development products.

Solutions. In-depth technical information and selected case studies that demonstrate how to build solutions with Microsoft tools and technologies.

Technology & Strategy. Strategic overview of Microsoft client-server and database technologies for enterprise-wide application development.

Resources. Overview of online services, publications, and other resources available to developers who write applications for the Windows and Windows NT operating systems.

Library Sampler. Selections from the current issue of the Development Library.

Platform Overview. Introduction to the Development Platform CD-ROM, and a complete listing of operating systems, SDKs, and toolkits on the latest version of the Platform.

In the Library Sampler area, we post selections from the Development Library along with new articles and updates. Click a title from the left pane to view an article. Click the button(s) in the article to download sample code from a bulletin board.

You can also use the Chat and Member-to-Member BBS sections to share information with other developers, Developer Kiosk to see an introduction to the Developer Network forum, and Feedback to send us your comments and suggestions.

First the hors d'oeuvres, later the entrée

Please consider this first attempt a prototype, and pardon the meager diet currently posted. We will be fleshing out and refining the Developer Network forum in the coming months.

Before we take the next step, though, we need your help: If you have a copy of the Windows 95 pre-release, please take a look at the Developer Network forum and tell us what you think. Are we on the right track? Are we giving you the information you need?

Also, if you're interested in creating content for MSN, you may be pleased to learn that a new online publishing environment is currently in the works (see "Microsoft Hatches 'Blackbird'").
