Developer Network OffRamp Debuts on Internet

Aim is to provide a greater quantity of timely information

Andrew Himes
Group Manager, Developer Network

The folks who bring you the Developer Network News, the Development Library, and the Development Platform have inaugurated a new information service on the World Wide Web (a graphical information-browsing system on the Internet): the Developer Network OffRamp.

We've been working on this plan for some time now. Developers have been telling us that they want more timely information from Microsoft, and they want us to make full use of the capabilities of the Internet for communicating and publishing. Our response is the OffRamp, your place to get via the Web the most up-to-date news and technical strategy information from Microsoft.

 

You can access the Developer Network OffRamp via Microsoft's World Wide Web server at http://www.microsoft.com. Once on the Microsoft Home Page, click the Developer Network OffRamp button.

 

The OffRamp includes three major areas:

• Developer Network News, an online version of our newspaper, updated weekly. (See Taking the Developer Network News Online.)

• Fast Track, a source of technical and strategic information, updated every two weeks.

• Developer Network membership information.

Timeliness and flexibility on the Internet

Publishing on the Internet provides us with several major benefits compared to print- or CD-based publishing. For one thing, we don't have the same space constraints. The paper edition of our newspaper that you are now reading is limited to 16 pages, and it's a challenge each month for us to fit the most essential and interesting articles into the newspaper. We invariably are forced to cut or postpone printing several articles. On the Web, however, we are limited only by disk space (which is relatively cheap) and the work it takes to make quantities of information easily usable and navigable.

Timeliness is an important factor in publishing, and the Web will help us get the right information to you in a much shorter period of time. Our publishing schedules for the Development Library on CD (every three months) and the Developer Network News on paper (every two months) are lengthy by comparison with our publishing schedule for the Web.

We continually update material on the OffRamp and add new content, so you can be sure that whatever is there is timely and appropriate. Each item is dated, so you know exactly when we added it to the OffRamp. In addition, all of the most important new material on the OffRamp is also included in the next available release of the Development Library on CD.

To obtain the Development Library, sign up for a Level 1 membership in the Developer Network. A one-year subscription to Level 1 costs US$195 ($275 CAN), which buys you four quarterly releases of the Library CD-ROM, chock full of technical information, plus six issues of the Developer Network News.

A Level 2 membership delivers all the benefits of Level 1, plus four quarterly releases of the Development Platform, which contains Microsoft API-level software development and device driver kits and the currently released versions (both U.S. and international) of the Windows and Windows NT Workstation operating systems for development and testing purposes. A one-year Level 2 membership costs US$495 ($695 CAN).

To join the Developer Network, call (800) 759-5474. Outside the United States and Canada, call the local Microsoft subsidiary.

Additional Information

Developer Network information

The Developer Network information area on the OffRamp provides up-to-date membership news and is designed to answer questions such as the following:

• How do I join the Developer Network?

• What content will be added to the next release of the Development Library?

• What new operating systems or toolkits will appear on the next Development Platform release?

• How do I change the membership options I've selected?

• What are the licensing restrictions for the Development Library and Development Platform?

Taking the Developer Network News online

The Developer Network's Web-based newspaper contains all the content of the paper-based version, plus a lot more. You can see all the news articles, columns, and features from past printed versions (including the most recent one), and in the "Extra" section you can also see new content added each week, news stories, features, and new technical columns, even before they appear in the printed newspaper.

Developer Network Fast Track

The Fast Track area contains technical and strategic information, including selected technical specifications, white papers, backgrounders, conference papers, selected samples and tools, information about developer tools and products from Microsoft, and the Microsoft Developer Knowledge Base.

Note: The Fast Track area is only a sampling of the much larger universe of content on the Development Library CD.
