Bad Hair Day: Resetting Windows NT3.5 Auto Logon

Have you ever bought a really cool, expensive gadget to do something that you could have done with a 25-cent gadget? But you used the cool, expensive gadget because it was new and, hey, you didn't know about the 25-cent one? Well, that's what about three thousand of you wrote to tell me.

Specifically, I gave a series of detailed steps to disable auto logon in Windows NT when another, easier solution was to hold down the shift key while you click the Logoff button. Now, don't all write to me asking for shirts, because the first one who sent this clever solution to me is the one who got the shirt. This is his letter:

Dear Dr. GUI:

I couldn't help but notice the Dr. GUI question about setting Windows NT auto logon when you've forgotten to give yourself administrative privileges. The same thing happened to me last month; luckily I didn't have to follow all the steps you listed. There appears to be a back door—if you hold down the shift key while logging off, it brings up the logon dialog box instead of doing an auto logon. I never found this documented anywhere, and it may be just a Windows NT 3.5 thing since I haven't tried it with Windows NT 3.1. But it's a lot easier!

Eric
