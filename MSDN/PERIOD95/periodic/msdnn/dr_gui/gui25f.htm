I Have DIBs on That!

Dear Dr. GUI:

I am working on a real-time simulation involving DIBs and need to combine two DIBs in memory, then put it on the screen. I have tried different versions of the SetDIBitsToDevice function and the BitBlt function. What is the fastest method of combining two DIBs in memory, and then copying it to the screen?

Thank you,
Kathleen Wilson

Dr. GUI replies:

The best way is to manipulate the bits of the DIBs (device-independent bitmaps) directly yourself. Since you know where the memory is, and the organization of a DIB is published in the SDK documentation, it's a simple matter to get a pointer to any pixel. Having combined your images using whatever composition algorithm you choose, you can copy the composite image to the screen using StretchDIBits with the DIB_RGB_COLORS option. This is by no means the fastest way, but it is simple. You should note that if you combine images, you'll also need to combine their color tables.
