WD98: Style Name Cut Off in Style Drop-down List

 Last reviewed: February 17, 1998
Article ID: Q178598
The information in this article applies to:
  • Microsoft Word 98 Macintosh Edition

SYMPTOMS

When you create a long style name or a style that is formatted with expanded characters, that style name may be cut off in the Style drop-down list on the Formatting toolbar.

CAUSE

The style name is too long or the expanded character formatting setting is too large.

WORKAROUND

To see the entire style name on the Style list, use the Organizer to shorten the name of the style. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. On the Format menu, click Style, and then click Organizer.

  2. Under Document name, select the style that has a long style name, and then click Rename.

  3. Type in a new, shortened name for the style, and then click OK.

  4. If this style appears in the other window of the Organizer and you want to change it in that template, click the style name and then click Rename.

  5. Type in a new, shortened name for the style, and then click OK.

  6. Click Close.

If the style has been formatted with expanded characters, you may want to decrease or remove that formatting. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. On the Format menu, click Style, and then under Styles, select the style in question.

  2. Click Modify, click Format, and then click Font. Click the Character Spacing tab.

  3. Beside Spacing, decrease the Expanded point size or change Expanded to Normal.

    NOTE: The Normal setting for Spacing removes the character spacing format.

  4. Click OK twice and then click Close.

STATUS

Microsoft has confirmed this to be a problem in the Microsoft products listed at the beginning of this article. We are researching this problem and will post new information here in the Microsoft Knowledge Base as it becomes available.
