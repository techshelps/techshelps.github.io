XADM: Service Advertising Protocol (SAP) ID and Descriptions

 Last reviewed: February 4, 1998
Article ID: Q179795
The information in this article applies to:
  • Microsoft Exchange Server, versions 4.0, 5.0, 5.5

SUMMARY

Service Advertising Protocols (SAPs) are used to broadcast information about available services on the network to other networked devices. A server may send out an SAP every 60 seconds to inform other network devices about the services it offers. Workstations use SAPs to find services they need on the network. The list of SAP IDs below corresponds to the SAP IDs that networking companies give to the services on the devices they produce. Filtering of these SAPs will reduce WAN traffic but may also prevent remote users across the router from using the filtered service.
