Setting a Cursor’s Hot Spot

Home |  Overview |  How Do I

The hot spot of a cursor is the point to which Windows refers in tracking the cursor’s position. By default, the hot spot is set to the upper-left corner (coordinates 0,0). The Cursor Properties page and the graphics editor control bar show the hot spot coordinates.

To set a cursor’s hot spot

  1. On the control bar of the Image editor window, choose the Hot Spot button.

  2. Click the pixel you want to designate as the cursor’s hot spot.
