Building a New Web Site

The World Wide Web is a great way for anyone with Internet access to communicate with the world. While you have conversations with other people over Internet e-mail and in Internet newsgroups, you can also have your own personal Web site — a collection of one or more pages on which you can share all sorts of information.

Your Web site can be about you, your interests, or a particular subject that you enjoy. For professional Web sites and intranets, you can share information about your company’s products and services, and you can make internal information more accessible to the employees in your organization.

You can place text, pictures, video clips, sounds, and animations on your Web pages. No matter how large or small your Web site will be, building it is easy with Microsoft FrontPage.

In this lesson, you will learn how to:

  • Launch Microsoft FrontPage

  • Construct a new FrontPage web

  • Apply a FrontPage theme

  • Design, edit, and save pages

  • Insert images

  • Create hyperlinks from text

  • Preview and save your work
