Appendix B. Han Compatibility Mappings

A set of Han Ideographic Compatibility to Han Ideographic mappings was given in The Unicode Standard, vol. 2, within the Cross-Reference Charts (pp. 382–385). Those mappings were, however, in error, as they were based on a pre-publication version of Unicode. (The mappings are indicated by a small, four-digit hexadecimal number under the code point.) The corrected mappings are listed below.

The characters within the Han Ideographic Compatibility zone of Unicode 1.1 come from three sources:

  • U+F900 ® U+FA0B are duplicate characters. KSC 5601-1989 encodes characters phonetically. Because this results in a number of duplicate characters, CJK-JRG moved them to the compatibility zone. For example, U+8C48 (KSC 4934) and U+F900 (KSC 4348) are the same ideograph—but have different pronunciations.
  • U+FA0C and U+FA0D are duplicate characters accidentally included within the Big Five character set.
  • U+FA0E ® U+FA2D are characters from industry standards—not national standards—that were required by Unicode developers. The Unified Repertoire and Ordering developed by the CJK-JRG and used by Unicode as the Han Ideographic zone was based on national standards only, and characters not in any of the national standards used were excluded. To satisfy the needs of Unicode developers, these 32 characters were added to the Han Ideographic Compatibility zone instead.
As characters U+F900 ® U+FA0D are identical to characters within the Han Ideographic zone, the mappings given here are from one character to an exact copy of the same character. U+FA0E ® U+FA2D are not identical to characters within the Han Ideographic zone. The mappings given here indicate possible unifications with characters within the Han Ideographic zone.
