New Names for Some Old MSDN Friends

by Jonathan King
Editor in Chief, Developer Network News

The offers from the Microsoft Developer Network (MSDN) were beginning to look like an archaeological dig. First there was just the Development Library, a.k.a. Level 1. Then we added the Development Platform—presto!—Level 2. A little while ago, we threw the BackOffice Test Platform into the mix and brilliantly called it Level 3.

So many levels would have made a guy like Heinrich Schliemann very happy, but MSDN members weren’t so amused. In fact, lots of people told us the naming scheme was downright labyrinthine.

OK, maybe we’re slow, but we’re not insensitive. So, beginning in April we will be making some changes to MSDN. Our goal is to give you a better understanding of what each membership level provides and which level of membership best meets your individual (or corporate) needs.

This means, starting with the April release, that the name of your MSDN membership will become consistent with the names of the Microsoft development tools you license. For example, if you use the "Enterprise" edition of Visual Basic, you’d probably want to have an MSDN "Enterprise Subscription."

Here’s a rundown of what you get with each level (oops, that word is out!) of the Microsoft Developer Network:

MSDN Library Subscription (formerly MSDN Level 1): The MSDN Library gives you product documentation, technical articles, sample code, the Developer Knowledge Base, and much more, plus you get bimonthly issues of the Developer Network News, two phone-support incidents, and a 20-percent discount on Microsoft Press books.

MSDN Professional Subscription (formerly MSDN Level 2): In addition to the MSDN Library and all the other benefits of the Library Subscription, the Professional Subscription gives you quarterly updates of the MSDN Development Platform, which contains all the SDKs, DDKs, Windows and Windows NT Workstation operating systems, plus premium shipments whenever there is an important "off-cycle" release.

MSDN Enterprise Subscription (formerly MSDN Level 3): With this level of membership, you get all the benefits of the Professional Subscription, plus quarterly updates of the BackOffice Test Platform, which contains the latest released versions of the six server components of Microsoft BackOffice, as well as special premium shipments of important off-cycle releases.

Please? Pretty please????

Now, a personal plea. I get numerous letters from folks asking me to change their addresses or sign them up for a subscription. I send these requests on to our operations people who, in turn, send them somewhere else to be entered into a database. (You can tell how much I understand about business operations.)

In the interest of streamlining, we ask you to cut out these middle steps by sending change-of-address notifications, renewal requests, and new subscription requests directly to the address in the box on this page.

Of course, you can keep sending me mail with suggestions and criticisms. Years of therapy have made me strong enough to withstand the blows to my ego.
