[Capital]

The capitalization rules tell the system when to capitalize the first letter of a word automatically, basically to capitalize at the beginning of the sentence.

Each line is a new rule, and rules are evaluated from first to last. Each rule contains one string (inverted) or a word, and an optional switch to turn it on/off.

Here are subset of the capitalization rules used for Voice Dictation English:

[Capital]

.\period

!\exclamation-point

?\question-mark

Mr.

Mrs.

Ms.

Dr.

Mme.

"\n"

"\r"

"\t"

"\"\n"

"\"\r"

"\"\t"

"\"."

"\"?"

"\"!"

The first few rules are words, which indicate that the next word dictated after those words is to be capitalized.

The "\n", "\r", and "\t" rules cause capitalization to occur on new lines and after tabs.
