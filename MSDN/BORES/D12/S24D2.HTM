X.400 Addressing

A management domain (MD) is a set of messaging systems managed by an organization that contains at least one message transfer agent. The management domain is divided into two parts: administrative management domain (ADMD) and private management domain (PRMD).

An ADMD is managed by a public service provider and is the highest level management domain that transmits third-party message traffic. The service is usually provided by a telephone carrier such as Sprint, AT&T, or British Telecom.

A PRMD is a network owned by a private company. PRMDs can communicate with ADMDs and other PRMDs but are not permitted to relay messages between PRMDs.

The ADMD and PRMD structure is similar to the telephone system. An ADMD can be compared to a telephone carrier that you receive phone service from. The service is leased from the ADMD in the form of a PRMD. This is similar to how a telephone line is assigned. The PRMD has a unique network address that can have multiple users.
